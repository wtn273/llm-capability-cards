LLM Capability Cards

A practical framework for deciding what to delegate to AI

Use these cards to identify which tasks are good candidates for LLM assistance and how to prompt for different types of cognitive work.

Explore the 17 Capabilities Generate Cards for Your Domain

Developed by the Human-AI Interaction Lab at UT Austin, these cards organize LLM capabilities using Bloom's taxonomy of cognitive processes—from simple retrieval to complex creation—helping you reason about task complexity before delegating to AI.

The Capability Cards

Our 17 capabilities are organized by Bloom's revised taxonomy of cognitive processes. The hierarchy shows how cognitive tasks build in complexity—from basic retrieval to complex creation. Click any card to view details, or filter by cognitive level.

The Method

Our two-phase co-design method helps practitioners generate grounded LLM application ideas:

1

Workflow Mapping

Practitioners map their existing workflows, identifying pain points and cognitive tasks they perform.

2

Capability Card Ideation

Using the capability cards, practitioners brainstorm how LLMs could support specific workflow tasks, then test ideas through prompt-based prototyping.
