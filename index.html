LLM Capability Cards

A cognitive framework for reasoning about LLM task delegation

We are researchers at the School of Information at UT Austin. We created a design resource to help practitioners reason about what to delegate to LLMs and how to approach different types of cognitive tasks.

Existing AI capability frameworks describe what systems do but don't help practitioners reason about task complexity. Our Bloom-aligned taxonomy organizes 17 LLM capabilities by cognitive level—from simple retrieval to complex creation—giving practitioners language to articulate which tasks to delegate versus preserve.

The Method

Our two-phase co-design method helps practitioners generate grounded LLM application ideas:

1

Workflow Mapping

Practitioners map their existing workflows, identifying pain points and cognitive tasks they perform.

2

Capability Card Ideation

Using the capability cards, practitioners brainstorm how LLMs could support specific workflow tasks, then test ideas through prompt-based prototyping.
